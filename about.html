Personal Story

My journey into systems started when I was 14. I started taking apart and rebuilding PCs, trying to understand how hardware and software actually worked together. I wasn't just interested in the screen—I was interested in the machine.

That curiosity for how systems really work never stopped. It just moved to a larger scale. Today, I don't build PCs; I build global cloud platforms for some of the world's most demanding organizations.

My Operating Philosophy

I believe that infrastructure is only useful if it's invisible. A perfect cloud platform is one that lets developers ship value without ever having to think about the underlying complexity. Security, scalability, and cost efficiency shouldn't be "features"—they should be the foundation.
